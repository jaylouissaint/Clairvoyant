Overview

Step 1: Calculating the Health Score

We began by developing a composite Health Score, a metric designed to measure financial “health” by integrating both performance (returns) and risk (volatility and drawdowns).
The score is calculated using the following formula:

Health Score
=
100 × (0.45𝑧(Excess 12M Return)
+ 0.35𝑧(Excess 3M Return)
− 0.10𝑧(Volatility)
−0.10𝑧(∣Max Drawdown∣))


Purpose of each term:

Excess 12-Month Return (45%) – Captures sustained performance relative to the S&P 500 benchmark (SPY).

Excess 3-Month Return (35%) – Reflects short-term momentum and emerging strength.

Volatility and Max Drawdown (20% total, negative weight) – Penalize instability and large price drops, rewarding steady performance.

Scaling by 100 – Converts the z-score composite into an interpretable index.

This weighting scheme ensures that the model prioritizes sustained growth while still recognizing recent performance and penalizing excessive risk.



Step 2: Applying the Metric Across Three Analytical Layers

The health score was consistently applied across three levels of granularity:

Industry-Level (ETF Proxies):
Using broad sector ETFs, each industry’s performance and risk were benchmarked against the S&P 500 to calculate its Industry Health Score. This identifies which sectors are outperforming the broader market.

Sub-Industry Level:
For each top-performing industry, the code retrieves the ETF’s  individual companies and aggregates their mean returns and median risk metrics. This identifies which sub-industries (e.g., Telecom Services, Semiconductors) drive each sector’s overall strength.

Company-Level:
At the most granular level, firm-specific 3- and 12-month returns, excess returns, and volatility are calculated relative to SPY. This allows for consistent comparison across companies and markets, producing a Company Health Score within each sub-industry.




Step 3: Identifying Top-Performing Industries

Once Health Scores were computed for all industries, the script automatically selects the top five industries based on their Industry Health Score.
Dynamic filtering (TOP5) ensures that the selection updates whenever the data or time horizon changes.



Step 4: Retrieving ETF Holdings and Sub-Industry Analysis

For each of the top industries, the script pulls the top holdings from ETF data via the Yahoo Finance API using a custom get_top_holdings() function.
This function attempts multiple retrieval methods (e.g., .fund_holdings, .get_fund_holdings(), .info) to ensure compatibility across ETF data sources.
The resulting company lists are then used to calculate Sub-Industry Health Scores using the same formula as the Industry level.

This stage determines which segments (such as “Internet Content & Information” within Communication Services) are driving industry performance.

Step 5: Identifying Industry Leaders

Following the Efficient Market Hypothesis, which assumes that a company’s market capitalization reflects all available public information, industry leaders were identified as the firms with the largest market capitalizations within each top-performing sub-industry.
This method ensures that our focus remains on financially sound, stable, and well-regarded firms that are representative of broader market strength.

These firms are most likely to align with ClAIrvoyant’s target profile—organizations with consistent growth, stability, and resources to invest in advanced talent acquisition technologies.

Step 6: Visual Analysis

After identifying the top industries and leading companies, we transitioned into visual exploration to validate findings and uncover additional insights:

Bar plots compare average health scores across industries.

Scatterplots visualize the relationship between return and volatility, highlighting sectors that balance growth and stability.

Heatmaps illustrate relationships between hiring activity (e.g., job postings) and financial strength.

Correlation matrices reveal the association between popularity indicators (LinkedIn followers, views) and financial health metrics.

These visuals collectively help determine whether market “hype” aligns with actual performance, a crucial consideration in selecting viable anchor clients.
